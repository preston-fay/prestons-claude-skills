Kearney Top 10
Highest-impact skills for technical colleagues doing analysis, delivery, and client work
Tier 1 — Kearney Essentials
1
Boris — High-Integrity Engineering
/boris
Activate spec-first, TDD, strongly-typed engineering discipline
A full engineering discipline mode: anti-injection guardrails, spec-before-code, types-before-logic, test-before-implementation. Includes 7 workflow commands (new-project, verify-app, test-and-fix, review-changes, quick-commit, commit-push-pr, add-rule).
Why it makes the top 10: The fastest way to write software is to write it right the first time. Boris mode enforces the discipline that makes that possible — for everyone on the team.
2
KDS Design Engine
/kds-design
Enforce Kearney brand standards across all UI and visual output
Universal Design Programme Engine (UDPE). Enforces KDS brand invariants: 8px grid, Major Third type scale, WCAG AA contrast, token discipline, spatial rhythm. Auto-applies to any UI generation, dashboard, slide, or visualization task.
Why it makes the top 10: Brand consistency at scale requires rules, not judgment. KDS-design makes brand compliance automatic — every chart, dashboard, and deliverable looks Kearney without extra effort.
3
Observable Framework
/observable-framework
Build interactive Kearney-branded dashboards and data presentations
Creates Observable Framework dashboards with 16:9 slide presentations, D3-powered visualizations, and executive-ready insights. Outputs are web-native, interactive, and Kearney-branded out of the box.
Why it makes the top 10: Static charts in decks are dead the moment they're printed. Observable dashboards let clients explore the data themselves — and they look unmistakably Kearney.
Tier 2 — The Analysis Chain
4
Exploratory Data Analysis
/eda
Profile datasets and surface patterns before analysis begins
Generates statistical summaries, distribution plots, correlation matrices, missing data maps, and anomaly flags for CSV, Excel, Parquet, or database inputs. First step on any unfamiliar dataset.
Why it makes the top 10: You can't analyze what you don't understand. EDA surfaces data quality issues and unexpected patterns before they corrupt your conclusions.
5
Analyze
/analyze
Extract key patterns and generate actionable recommendations
Goes beyond EDA to synthesize findings into business insights. Identifies top drivers, segments, and anomalies — then creates an executive summary with prioritized recommendations.
Why it makes the top 10: EDA tells you what the data looks like. Analyze tells you what it means and what to do about it. That's the transition from analysis to advice.
6
Story Architect
/story-architect
Build data narratives that change how people think
Diagnoses audience cognitive biases using Factfulness instincts, structures narratives with Rosling's 5-phase dramatic arc, and builds persuasive stories with progressive revelation and surprise pivots.
Why it makes the top 10: The difference between analysis that gets acted on and analysis that gets shelved is almost always the story. This skill forces the right narrative structure before a single slide gets made.
7
Hans Rosling Reference
hans-rosling/
Comprehensive reference on the world's greatest data storyteller
Deep analysis of Rosling's methods: animated bubble charts, Misconception→Data→Surprise→Hope narrative arc, Factfulness instincts, and the physical theatrics that made data feel like a spectator sport.
Why it makes the top 10: Rosling's methods are the gold standard for data communication. Understanding them analytically — not just watching them — lets you apply the principles deliberately, not accidentally.
Tier 3 — Consulting Output
8
Executive Summary Writer
/exec-summary
Distill complex analysis into compelling 1-page summaries
Uses SCQA (Situation-Complication-Question-Answer) and Pyramid Principle to structure executive summaries. Leads with the recommendation, not the journey. Built for senior audiences with low reading patience.
Why it makes the top 10: Most analysts bury the lead. SCQA forces the answer first, which is what every executive actually wants — and what every Kearney deliverable should deliver.
9
PowerPoint (pptx)
/pptx
Create, modify, and analyze .pptx presentations programmatically
Supports creating new presentations, editing existing ones, working with slide layouts, adding speaker notes and comments. Can batch-process slides and apply consistent formatting at scale.
Why it makes the top 10: PowerPoint is the delivery format for virtually every Kearney client output. Programmatic generation beats manual formatting when working with data-driven content or templates.
10
Bayesian Causal Discovery
/bayesian-causal-discovery
Discover causal relationships in data — not just correlations
Runs Bayesian causal structure learning on tabular data to produce a directed acyclic graph (DAG), bootstrap stability analysis, and confidence-tiered edge findings. Works on CSV, SQLite, or pandas DataFrames.
Why it makes the top 10: Correlation tells you what moves together. Causality tells you what to change. For root-cause and driver analysis on client engagements, this is the difference between a recommendation and a guess.
Strategy & Consulting 11 skills
Story Architect

Storytell
/storytell
Quick narrative templates and writing techniques
Provides proven story structures (Problem-Solution, Journey, Comparison), writing techniques (lead with insight, quantify, create contrast), and report templates for exec summaries, one-pagers, and slide outlines.
Why: A consistent structure shaves hours off write-ups and forces you to answer "so what?" before you start writing.
Issue Tree
/issue-tree
Decompose complex problems into MECE issue trees
Breaks any business question into 3–4 levels of mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive sub-issues. Forces logical completeness and reveals where the real question lives.
Why: Most bad analysis attacks the wrong problem. An issue tree prevents that by making the problem structure explicit before any work begins.
Hypothesis Tree
/hypothesis-tree
Build structured hypothesis trees with testable sub-hypotheses
Converts a business question into a hierarchy of falsifiable hypotheses, each with a defined test and success criterion. Ensures analysis is driven by hypotheses, not just data exploration.
Why: Hypothesis-first thinking is 3–5× faster than open-ended exploration because you know exactly what you're looking for and when to stop.
Insight Synthesizer
/insight-synth
Transform raw analysis into meaningful, actionable insights
Applies the "So What?" technique to convert observations into insights. Forces every finding to answer what it means, why it matters, and what to do about it.
Why: Raw findings aren't insights — insights are findings plus implication. This skill does the translation automatically.
Growth & Efficiency Matrix
/growth-matrix
Classify business units into strategic quadrants
Places business units into Stars, Cash Cows, Question Marks, and Turnarounds using growth and profitability axes. Generates portfolio strategy recommendations and prioritization rationale.
Why: A clear portfolio view prevents the trap of treating all businesses the same — different quadrants need fundamentally different strategies.
Value Chain Diagnostic
/value-chain
Find margin leakage across the value chain
Analyzes cost, revenue, and margin by value chain stage. Surfaces where value is created vs. destroyed and builds the fact base for where to focus improvement efforts.
Why: Margin improvement without a value chain view is guesswork. This skill makes the invisible visible before any recommendations are made.
Market Sizing
/market-sizing
Size markets using TAM / SAM / SOM frameworks
Builds both top-down (industry data → segment share) and bottom-up (unit economics × addressable buyers) market size estimates. Triangulates and surfaces key assumptions.
Why: Clients use market size to justify investments. Two approaches that converge make the estimate defensible under scrutiny.
